Every portrait is a world you haven't seen yet.

Shelly Mosman makes photographs that stop people. Her subjects — children draped in cranes, musicians wreathed in smoke, families staged like Old Masters with living animals — exist in a space between reality and something far more interesting. Working from her studio inside the Casket Arts Building in Northeast Minneapolis, she constructs elaborate narrative tableaux using vintage costumes, taxidermy, live animals, hand-painted backdrops, and a painter's instinct for light.

Trained in drawing and painting at the Minneapolis College of Art and Design, Mosman approaches photography the way a director approaches a film — every prop placed, every gesture considered, every frame built to hold a story. Her signature series Animal Child and Mother Child have become some of the most recognized portrait work coming out of the Midwest, blending the theatrical grandeur of European art history with an irreverence that is entirely her own.

Her work is held in the permanent collections of the Plains Art Museum, Eiteljorg Museum, and Rockford Art Museum. She has been published internationally in Germany, France, Australia, Brazil, and the United States, awarded 1st Prize in the LensCulture Exposure Awards, exhibited at Art Basel Miami / Aqua, and shown at the Minnesota Marine Art Museum, Rockford Art Museum, and Circa Gallery.

She co-created The Conscious Kingdom with Tina Wilcox — a fine art book in which Wilcox curated and costumed a collection of humanely procured taxidermy in Victorian regalia, and Mosman brought each character to life through photography. A portion of proceeds benefits the Animal Humane Society.

Prints, commissions, and original works are available. Mosman works with private collectors, art consultants, galleries, and editorial clients worldwide.
