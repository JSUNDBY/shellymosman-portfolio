The portrait is never just a picture. It's a world.

Shelly Mosman is a Minneapolis-based portrait photographer whose work sits at the intersection of fine art and narrative storytelling. Working from her 1,500-square-foot studio inside the Casket Arts Building in Northeast Minneapolis, she creates images that blur the line between reality and something stranger, more tender, more alive.

A graduate of the Minneapolis College of Art and Design with a background in drawing and painting, Shelly brings a painter's eye to every frame. Her studio — seven shooting bays, wet and dry darkrooms, and a menagerie of props including taxidermy, vintage costumes, and live animals — is a stage where the ordinary becomes extraordinary.

Over two decades, her work has evolved from commercial and wedding photography into a deeply personal practice of expressive, imaginative portraiture. Her signature series — Animal Child, Mother Child, and her black-and-white portraits — weave together influences from European art history and modern pop culture, capturing subjects in moments that feel both timeless and utterly present.

Her photographs have been published internationally in Germany, France, Australia, Brazil, and the United States. She was awarded 1st Prize in the LensCulture Exposure Awards portfolio category, exhibited at Art Basel Miami / Aqua, and shown at the Minnesota Marine Art Museum, Rockford Art Museum, and Circa Gallery. Her work lives in the permanent collections of the Plains Art Museum, Eiteljorg Museum, and Rockford Art Museum.

She also co-created The Conscious Kingdom, a fine art book with Tina Wilcox, who curated and styled a collection of humanely procured taxidermy in Victorian costuming — with Shelly bringing the vision to life through photography. A portion of proceeds benefits the Animal Humane Society.
